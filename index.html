Project Summary

This project develops a Google Earth Engine (GEE)–based application for researchers and human rights monitors to systematically identify and explore spatial patterns of suspected scam compounds in Southeast Asia, specifically across the Cambodia–Vietnam corridor, Myanmar–Thailand border, and the Golden Triangle region. Referencing data from Amnesty International and ASPI, the tool extracts remote sensing indicators of involuntary detention infrastructure to screen for high-risk areas. Using 53 confirmed locations as a reference sample, the project employs a two-stage similarity-based analysis to identify 24,464 candidate zones with similar physical characteristics, refined to a shortlist of 15 high-priority locations. The interactive web platform enhances spatial communication and supports collaborative documentation, helping governments and law enforcement agencies combat criminal networks and protect national border security.

Problem Statement

Scam compounds are difficult to systematically detect due to:

  • Limited verified ground-truth data
  • Rapid spatial development in border regions
  • Overlap with legitimate urban and commercial environments
  • Inconsistent reporting across NGO and governmental sources

This project supports researchers and policy-oriented users by providing a reproducible, satellite-based screening tool grounded in documented reference cases, rather than making definitive claims of confirmed presence.

End User

Our platform is designed for human rights monitoring agencies, investigative journalists, and policy-oriented researchers working to combat transnational crime in Southeast Asia. Limited public information and complex on-the-ground situations have long hampered the effectiveness of international interventions. Our interactive maps significantly enhance data accessibility and applicability, fostering collaboration among global stakeholders and providing robust spatial evidence to support international initiatives against forced labour, human trafficking, and torture.

Methodology

The analysis pipeline operates in two stages:

Stage 1 — Satellite Embedding Similarity

A Sentinel-2 median composite (2024, 20 m resolution) is used to compute per-pixel spectral similarity against the 53 confirmed reference sites. Areas exceeding the p97 similarity threshold are retained as Stage 1 candidates, covering approximately 3.7% of the Cambodia–Vietnam AOI while capturing all suspected sites and non-reference confirmed sites within 500 m.

\[\text{Similarity} = \frac{\sum_{i} w_i \cdot \cos(\mathbf{e}_i, \mathbf{r}_i)}{\sum_{i} w_i}\]

Stage 2 — Indicator-Based Refinement

Candidate zones are scored against a metrics stack:

Indicator Description
dNDBI 2021–2024 Built-up area growth: \(\text{NDBI} = \frac{\text{SWIR} - \text{NIR}}{\text{SWIR} + \text{NIR}}\)
dNTL 2021–2024 Night-time light radiance growth
dNDVI 2021–2024 Vegetation loss
dist_to_confirmed_m Proximity to known confirmed sites
dist_to_border_m Proximity to national borders

The Application

How it Works

The application is built entirely in Google Earth Engine and operates through a two-stage screening pipeline. Below are key code examples illustrating the core logic.

Stage 1: Computing NDBI Change

// Compute NDBI for 2021 and 2024 Sentinel-2 composites
var s2_2021 = ee.ImageCollection('COPERNICUS/S2_SR_HARMONIZED')
  .filterBounds(aoi)
  .filterDate('2021-01-01', '2021-12-31')
  .filter(ee.Filter.lt('CLOUDY_PIXEL_PERCENTAGE', 30))
  .select(['B11', 'B8'])
  .median();

var ndbi2021 = s2_2021.normalizedDifference(['B11', 'B8']).rename('NDBI');
var ndbi2024 = s2_2024.normalizedDifference(['B11', 'B8']).rename('NDBI');

// dNDBI: positive values indicate built-up growth
var dNDBI = ndbi2024.subtract(ndbi2021).rename('dNDBI');

Stage 2: Candidate Tier Classification

// Candidates are scored and classified into tiers
// High: both development_flag and activity_flag triggered
// Medium: one flag triggered  
// Low: neither flag, retained as lower-confidence candidates
var highCandidates   = candidates.filter(ee.Filter.eq('priority_tier', 'high'));
var mediumCandidates = candidates.filter(ee.Filter.eq('priority_tier', 'medium'));
var lowCandidates    = candidates.filter(ee.Filter.eq('priority_tier', 'low'));

Candidate Density Visualisation

// Paint candidate polygons at 1 km scale and apply smoothing kernel
var painted = ee.Image().byte().paint(candidates, 1)
  .setDefaultProjection('EPSG:3857', null, 1000);

var kernel = ee.Kernel.square({radius: 12000, units: 'meters', normalize: false});

var densityImage = painted
  .reduceNeighborhood({reducer: ee.Reducer.sum(), kernel: kernel, optimization: 'boxcar'})
  .resample('bilinear')
  .clip(aoi)
  .selfMask();

The Stage 2 pipeline reduces 24,464 Stage 1 candidates to a shortlist of 15 high-priority zones by applying distance and night-time light thresholds:

Tier Count Criteria
High 8,425 Both development and activity flags triggered
Medium 13,059 One flag triggered
Low 2,980 Neither flag
Operational shortlist 15 Strictest distance and NTL conditions
